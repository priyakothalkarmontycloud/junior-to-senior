# Mission: Strategic Programming in the AI Era

## Why
Priya is transitioning from tactical programming (writing code) to strategic programming
(deciding what to build, how to build it, and why). AI tools are rapidly automating
on-the-ground coding, making the strategic, architectural, and planning skills — those that
historically separated junior from senior developers — the new table stakes for any developer
who wants to remain highly effective and highly valuable.

## Success looks like
- Writing a technical scope document before any implementation begins, as a reflex
- Leading architectural discussions and producing ADRs that shape how teams build
- Designing systems that can scale, adapt, and be understood without the original author present
- Running a QA strategy that tests the right things, not just the obvious things
- Making build-vs-buy-vs-AI decisions confidently and with written justification
- Directing AI tools effectively: giving them precise, bounded, testable instructions

## Constraints
- Primary context is a cloud management SaaS (MontyCloud) — lessons grounded here where possible
- Learning happens in focused sessions alongside active development work
- Preference for immediately actionable skills over abstract theory

## Out of scope
- Low-level language mastery (hot-path optimization, memory management)
- DevOps / infrastructure operations (separate domain)
- Engineering management and people skills (out of scope for now)
